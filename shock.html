Hemodynamic Pharmacology / Compartment Model

Why we cut the dose.

When MAP drops, the peripheral compartment closes off. Drug pools in a smaller central volume — and a larger fraction of that flow goes to the brain. Same dose, much higher effect.

Clinical Pearl
At a normal MAP, the dose distributes across roughly 3.0 L/kg. Plasma and brain levels sit in the therapeutic window. Now drop the MAP and watch the math change.
Educational model only. Vd values are approximations from the ketamine pharmacokinetic literature; the shock-state Vd contraction reflects published reductions in central compartment volume during hypoperfusion (Persson 2002; Clements & Nimmo 1981; Hijazi et al. 2003). Brain exposure is modeled as a function of plasma concentration multiplied by relative cerebral blood flow fraction during sympathetic shunting. Not a dosing calculator.
